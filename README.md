# House of Dice

You've been invited to a game. Don't read too much into who sent it.

There's a room behind a door that isn't on any floor plan. Green felt,
one lamp, a dealer who counts faster than you think and blinks less than
you'd like. The game is dice — five of them, the honest kind, at least
when you arrive. You roll, you keep what you like, you write the rest
into a ledger that only turns its page when the house says so.

Beat the number on the table and the house pays you. Spend it at the
counter: dice carved into liars, gold that works for you, glass that
counts double right up until it doesn't. Every third table, a regular
sits down across from you. They have names. They have rules. They have
all the time you don't.

Eight acts. The last chair is empty. It plays anyway.

**To play:** open it in your browser — [take a seat here](https://ordodox.github.io/houseofdice/)
— or grab `index.html` and open it straight from disk. That's it. No
install. It works with the internet off, which is also how the house
prefers things.

**To stop playing:** that's been tried.

A few tips the dealer would never give you: read the map before you
build. The ledger only refreshes between acts — sometimes you must
write a zero into something you loved. And if a stranger between tables
offers you a die, remember that everything in this place was someone's.

The chair is warm. Don't ask.
